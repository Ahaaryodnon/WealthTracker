5. What Could the Top 350's £772.8 Bn Fund?

0.4% (£3 bn/yr) — Scrap the two-child benefit cap and lift 550,000 children out of poverty

The cost of child poverty to the UK economy is £39 billion/year — 13× the cost of scrapping the cap. (CPAG 2023, Save the Children 2026)

0.3% (£2.5 bn/yr) — Universal free school meals for every child in England

Currently 4.3 million children in food-insecure households (Hansard 2024).

1.3% (£10 bn/yr) — Build 90,000 social homes per year

1.3 million households on waiting lists. At current build rates: 119-year wait to clear (Shelter 2024).

5% (£39 bn) — Wipe out the annual economic cost of child poverty

The drag on the UK economy from child poverty alone — lost productivity, extra public spending — is £39 bn/year.

31% (£242 bn) — Fund the entire NHS for one year

NHS expenditure 2024/25. The Hinduja family alone could fund it for 53 days.

100% (£772.8 bn) — Run the NHS for 3.2 years. Or fund the UK's entire defence budget for 14 years. Or give every UK household £27,600.

6. The Historical Theft

"A 2% annual wealth tax on the Rich List since the mid-1990s would have raised £325 billion — over £11,000 per UK household — and the rich would STILL have got richer." — Dr Benjamin Tippet, King's College London
The UK's wealth gap has grown by 50% in just 8 years. Most wealth increases have been "passive" (property appreciation), not earned — and the lion's share has gone to those already in the top 10%. (LSE Inequalities, 2024)
