# Product Brief: WealthTracker

## Executive Summary

WealthTracker is a public-facing inequality exposure tool that transforms how people understand extreme wealth. Unlike traditional rich lists that show static net worth figures, WealthTracker reveals the **passive income** billionaires generate — making visible the money they earn every second without lifting a finger.

The core insight: people know billionaires are rich, but they don't *feel* it. By showing that a billionaire earns an average person's annual salary in minutes, WealthTracker creates visceral understanding that static numbers cannot. The goal is to shift public perception toward support for wealth taxation by letting the mathematics of inequality speak for itself.

**The centrepiece: a live Accumulator** showing the combined passive income of the world's 10 richest people — ticking up in real-time as you watch. This single mechanic delivers immediate gut-punch impact, inherent shareability, and a reason to return for milestone moments.

Positioned as "a calculator for inequality, not a celebrity tracker," WealthTracker aggregates multiple data sources into a single credible reference, shows averages with full transparency into underlying figures, and frames comparisons in relatable human terms — lifetime earnings, public service budgets, hours worked versus hours slept.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

The scale of billionaire wealth is invisible. Traditional rich lists present net worth as static figures — "$200 billion" — which are too abstract to comprehend. People intellectually know inequality exists, but lack the visceral understanding needed to drive action. The result: public opinion remains passive while the gap widens.

### Problem Impact

Without understanding how wealth compounds, the public underestimates both the scale of inequality and the potential impact of policy interventions like wealth taxation. Billionaires earn more while sleeping than most people will earn in a lifetime, yet this reality remains hidden behind incomprehensible numbers. The longer this gap in understanding persists, the harder meaningful change becomes.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

- **Rich lists (Forbes, Bloomberg)**: Show static net worth, not income flow. Framed as aspirational "who's winning" coverage rather than inequality exposure.
- **News coverage**: Occasional "Bezos made $13B today" headlines conflate stock price swings with passive income. No sustained, comparable methodology.
- **Academic/NGO reports**: Rigorous but inaccessible. Dense PDFs don't change minds at scale.
- **Viral comparisons**: Twitter threads with "$1 bill stacks" are momentarily engaging but lack credibility, sources, or persistence.

Nobody is showing the **steady, predictable, effortless income stream** from returns in a credible, accessible, permanent format.

### Proposed Solution

WealthTracker makes passive income visible through:

1. **The Accumulator**: Live counter showing combined passive income of the top 10 richest people — ticking up in real-time, with cumulative totals and "since you arrived" session counters
2. **Passive income calculations**: Model what billionaires earn per year/day/hour/minute using transparent return assumptions (3%/5%/7%)
3. **Relatable comparisons**: "Earns your annual salary every X minutes" — grounded in median wages, public budgets, lifetime earnings
4. **Multi-source aggregation**: Average figures from Forbes, Bloomberg, public filings — with full transparency into underlying data
5. **Milestone triggers**: Automatic comparisons when thresholds hit ("Just passed annual NHS nurse salary budget")
6. **Transparent methodology**: Show assumptions, ranges, uncertainty — credibility through honesty

### Key Differentiators

| Differentiator | Why It Matters |
|----------------|----------------|
| **The Accumulator** | Live, ticking counter creates visceral "inequality happening now" feeling — not static snapshots |
| **Passive income focus** | Nobody else shows the *flow* of wealth generation |
| **Multi-source transparency** | Show averages AND underlying data — more credible than single sources |
| **Relatable comparisons** | Translates billions into human terms: salaries, lifetimes, public services |
| **Milestone moments** | Built-in press opportunities and share triggers |
| **Credible but pointed** | Facts without moralizing; the math does the advocacy |

**The aha moment:** *"They make more asleep than I'll make in my lifetime."*

## MVP Scope

### Core Features

**One page. Maximum impact.**

| Feature | Description | Why Essential |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Live Accumulator** | Combined passive income of top 10 richest people, ticking up in real-time | The gut-punch — core value proposition |
| **"Since you arrived" counter** | Personal session counter showing earnings during visit | Makes inequality visceral and personal |
| **Year-to-date total** | Cumulative figure since January 1st | Provides shocking scale context |
| **Top 10 list** | Names, net worth, individual passive income rate | Shows who's driving the numbers |
| **Relatable comparisons** | "Earns your annual salary every X minutes" format | Translates billions into human terms |
| **Methodology section** | Transparent "how we calculate this" explanation | Establishes credibility |
| **Share functionality** | Easy social sharing buttons | Enables viral spread |

**Data requirements:**
- Top 10 billionaires by net worth (multi-source aggregated)
- Passive income calculations at 3%/5%/7% return assumptions
- Median salary data for comparison baseline
- Source citations and timestamps

| Interactive return assumption sliders | Complexity; most users want the default gut-punch | v2 |

| Embeddable widgets | Journalist feature, validate demand first | v2 |
